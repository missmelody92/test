Chinese
Superstitions

Many of us hear about superstitions from our older relatives. These stories can date back from as early from when our ancestors were around. Even today despite modern lifestyle and contemporary culture The people are still very superstitous. The Chinese New Year is the most important day to bring in prosperity. In almost every Chinese restaurant you will be greeted by a golden cat figure. This is a mascot for bringing wealth to the establishment. Have a look at these customs:

1 On the first day of The New Year, the house should not be cleaned. This is because, it is believed good ora would leave the home.
2 For good luck and to welcome the New Year, it is recommended to wear red clothes.
3 To welcome the New Year, open the windows of the house to allow the past year to leave.
4 When a baby is born their beauty should nt be praised, it is believed this will jinx their looks.
5 In Chinese fung Shui, the art of moving furniture. Everything must point North for good luck.
6 The number eight is lucky. However the number four represents bad luck. In Cantonese it is pronounced "Sei", which means Death.
7 Facial hair on men is considered unlucky.
8 Men and Woman with the same surname generally won't get married as the Chinese fear they may be related.
9 Wedding Dresses are custom to be White, Red or Yellow.
10 Nails should never be cut at night because it is believed to bring financial loss.
